Overview

What is Sentinel?

Sentinel is Xenolyth's flagship autonomous intelligence platform, purpose-built for Earth situational awareness. It ingests live data from NASA, space agencies, and multiple real-time APIs — processing, analysing, and presenting a unified operational picture of Earth's near-space environment.

Sentinel is currently in private beta. Access is granted exclusively through Xenolyth's application review process. The platform is designed for analysts, researchers, and operators who require reliable, real-time space intelligence.

Features

Core Capabilities

🌍

ISS Live Tracking

Real-time International Space Station telemetry including position, velocity, orbital path, and crew operations status.

☄️

Near-Earth Object Detection

Active monitoring of asteroids approaching Earth, with proximity radar, threat assessment, and detailed orbital data.

🚀

Launch Intelligence

Global rocket launch tracking with live countdowns, mission briefings, AI-generated trajectory analysis, and orbital destination data.

☀️

🤖

Autonomous AI Briefings

Daily and weekly AI-generated intelligence summaries synthesising all data streams into clear, actionable situational awareness.

FAQ

Common Questions

What is Sentinel exactly? +
Sentinel is an autonomous Earth situational awareness platform built by Xenolyth. It monitors near-Earth objects, ISS operations, rocket launches, and solar activity in real time, synthesised through AI into a unified operational intelligence interface.
How do I get access to Sentinel? +
Sentinel is currently in private beta. Access is not publicly available. Submit a beta request through this website. All applications are reviewed personally by Xenolyth, and selected applicants will be contacted with onboarding information.
Is there a cost to join the beta? +
No. The private beta is currently available at no cost. Beta participants help shape the direction of Sentinel through feedback and usage. Future pricing will be announced before any commercial release.
Who is Sentinel designed for? +
Sentinel is designed for researchers, analysts, aerospace enthusiasts, educators, and organisations who require real-time situational awareness of Earth's near-space environment. It is an intelligence platform for informed operators — not a general consumer product.
What data sources does Sentinel use? +
Sentinel integrates data from NASA's public APIs (APOD, NeoWs, DONKI), real-time ISS position feeds, global launch tracking services, and solar monitoring networks. All data is sourced from authoritative scientific organisations and updated continuously.
